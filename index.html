How to use this dashboard

This dashboard turns a complex skilled visa dataset into a practical decision tool. Whether you are a student, migrant, recruiter, employer, consultant, researcher, or content creator, you can use the filters and charts to quickly identify where grants are concentrated, which occupations are active, what sponsor industries are strongest, and how different countries, locations, and applicant types compare over time.

Why this dashboard is useful

  • Students and visa applicants can explore which occupations, industries, and states appear most active in the skilled visa dataset.
  • Recruiters and employers can identify concentration patterns by occupation, industry, and location.
  • Migration consultants can use it to support evidence-based discussions with clients.
  • Researchers and analysts can filter the dataset deeply and spot trends that are difficult to see in Excel alone.
  • Content creators and marketers can turn the data into attention-grabbing insights, state comparisons, trend posts, and occupation-focused narratives.

Best way to use it

  • Start with broad filters such as financial year, applicant type, and citizenship country.
  • Then narrow by state, SA3, SA4, occupation, sponsor industry, visa type, and subclass.
  • Use the Deep Dive tab to understand country, industry, occupation, and SA3 concentration.
  • Use the remove not applicable option when you want cleaner category charts.
  • Use the data table for a direct grouped summary after applying filters.

Marketing and content use

This dashboard is strong for storytelling. You can turn filtered views into useful posts like “Top occupations for Indian applicants in NSW”, “Which industries dominate subclass patterns”, or “How applicant trends changed over time”. The combination of filters and charts helps people move from raw data to clear, shareable insights fast.

Data Items and Terminology Used

ANZSIC Australian and New Zealand Standard Industry Classification 2006 Edition produced by the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS). Prior to November 2008, ANZSIC 1993 was used to record industry. Data recorded using ANZSIC 1993 has been converted to ANZSIC 2006 using concordance tables provided by the ABS.
Client Location Client location filter for In Australia means the application was lodged in Australia. Client location filter for Outside Australia means the application was lodged outside Australia.
Nominated Occupation (Major Group) Nominated Occupation Major Group in Department's Systems.
Nominated Position Location The geographic location where the visa holder will be working. This may be different from the business address of the sponsor and/or the visa processing office.
Sponsor Industry The industry self-identified by the sponsoring client using the Australian and New Zealand Standard Industry Classification (ANZSIC) 2006 edition.
Nominated Occupation The occupation of the nominated position that the primary visa holder will be filling in Australia while holding a Temporary Resident (Skilled) visa based on the Australian Standard Classification of Occupations (ANZSCO) 1st Edition.
Country of Citizenship Passport holder of a country.
Primary Applicant The primary applicant is the person who must satisfy the primary criteria for the grant of a visa under the Migration Regulations.
Secondary Applicant Secondary applicants must satisfy the secondary criteria for the grant of a visa under the Regulations. In this case, a secondary applicant is generally a dependant of the primary applicant.

Explanatory Notes

Data Sources

Data is sourced from Integrated Client Services Environment (ICSE) and Immigration Record and Information System (IRIS).

Business Rules

1. This report is based on visa grant data recorded for Temporary Resident (Skilled) visa subclasses in the current financial year and the previous financial year. It excludes a small number of visa grants made to Independent Executives, who also used to enter on Temporary Resident (Skilled) visas but are now included in the Business Skills categories.
2. ANZSCO was introduced in the department on 1 July 2010. Applications lodged prior to that date using the Australian Standard Classification of Occupations (ASCO), 2nd Edition have been converted to an ANZSCO code using a standard departmental mapping approved by the ABS.
3. Unless otherwise stated, the occupation shown in tables in this report is the nominated occupation. That is, the position that is being filled rather than the previous or usual occupation of the visa applicant. For secondary applicants, 'Not Applicable' is shown for the nominated occupation.
4. Sponsors self-identify with an industry classification when lodging a nomination or sponsorship. The industry classification shown is based on what the sponsor has recorded. If a sponsor has notified more than one industry over time, the most recently recorded industry classification has been used in this report. If the Temporary Resident (Skilled) visa holder has changed sponsor without lodging a new visa application, a small percentage of the industry classification may be based on the sponsor at the time of visa grant rather than the sponsor at the snapshot date. For secondary applicants, 'Not Applicable' is shown for the sponsor industry.
5. In some cases, 'Not recorded' or 'Not specified' appears. This mainly relates to visa applications processed in the IRIS system. IRIS does not have the capacity to record comprehensive information about sponsors and nominations. Since 1 November 2003, the ICSE system has largely replaced IRIS for processing Temporary Resident (Skilled) visas, but a small number of visas are still processed in IRIS.
6. From 14 September 2009, subclass 457 visa holders changing sponsor or nominated occupation do not need to lodge a new visa application if they held a valid Temporary Resident (Skilled) visa. The department estimates that the number of visa applications lodged since 14 September 2009 has been reduced by 8 to 9 per cent below the level that would have occurred without the change.
7. The age of the client in the table is based on the time at visa granted.
8. The nominated position location 'State' and the 'Statistical Area' (ASGS) of the secondary applicant is based on the primary applicant.
9. The nominated position location Statistical Area (ASGS) is based on the 'Postal Area 2016 to Statistical Area Level 3 2016' weighted correspondence mapping provided by the ABS.
